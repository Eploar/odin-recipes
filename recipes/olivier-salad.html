Olivier Salad

Description

Olivier salad is a traditional Balkans starter. It is a cold, creamy salad made with diced potatoes, carrots, peas, eggs, pickles, and sometimes chicken or ham, all mixed together with mayonnaise. The texture is chunky but soft, and the flavor is a mix of mild, slightly tangy, and creamy. It’s simple but very filling. it actually originated in 19th-century Moscow, created by a chef named Lucien Olivier, which is why it is also called Russian Salad

Ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Carrots
  • Peas
  • Eggs
  • Pickles
  • Cooked chicken or ham
  • Mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation

  1. Boil potatoes and carrots until tender, then let them cool
  2. Boil eggs separately, cool, and peel them
  3. Dice the potatoes, carrots, eggs, and pickles into small cubes
  4. If using meat, cook it first and chop it into bite-sized pieces
  5. Combine everything in a large bowl and add peas
  6. Mix in mayonnaise, then season with salt and pepper
  7. Stir gently until everything is evenly coated
  8. Chill in the fridge before serving for the best flavor
