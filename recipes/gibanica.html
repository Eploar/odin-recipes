Gibanica

Description

Gibanica is a traditional savory pastry from the Balkans, especially popular in Serbia and neighboring countries. It’s made with thin layers of filo dough filled with a mixture of white cheese (often similar to feta), eggs, and sometimes yogurt or sour cream. The layers are either neatly stacked or casually folded, giving it a slightly rustic, uneven texture. When baked, gibanica becomes golden and crispy on top while staying soft and rich inside.

Ingredients

  • Filo dough sheets
  • White cheese (feta or similar)
  • Eggs
  • Yogurt or sour cream
  • Cooking oil
  • A pinch of salt

Preparation

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C.
  2. In a bowl, mix together crumbled white cheese, a few eggs, and a bit of yogurt or sour cream until you get a creamy filling.
  3. Lightly oil a baking dish, then place a couple of sheets of filo dough on the bottom. Add some of the cheese mixture, then keep layering dough and filling until you use everything up.
  4. Finish with a couple of filo sheets on top and brush them with oil.
  5. Bake for around 30 to 40 minutes until the top turns golden brown.
  6. Let it cool slightly before cutting. It’s best served warm, when the inside is still soft and rich.
